Exam Question 4: Loss Surfaces and Convergence Challenges
Deep neural networks are optimized over complex, non-convex loss surfaces. Discuss the challenges associated with such loss surfaces by addressing:

The concepts of local minima, sharp minima versus wide minima, and how the geometry of the loss surface affects convergence and generalization.

How gradient descent (especially mini-batch SGD) and momentum can help navigate these loss landscapes and potentially escape poor local minima.

The role of noisy gradient estimates (from small batch sizes) in exploring the loss surface and their impact on converging to better solutions.
